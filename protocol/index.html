Daily stack — timing & rationale
Morning
With breakfast
Vitamin D3 + K2
4000 IU + 100mcg MK-7
D3 corrects near-certain deficiency in North Yorkshire — directly linked to brain fog, fatigue, low mood. K2 MK-7 directs calcium to bones not arteries. Fat-soluble — must take with food.
With food
Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)
2g combined
Brain function, inflammation, cardiovascular, mood. Triglyceride form (Bare Biology) — 70% more bioavailable than ethyl ester. Takes 8–12 weeks to see full cognitive effect.
With food
B Complex (methylated)
1 capsule
Methylcobalamin B12 + methylfolate B9 — active forms only. Stress depletes B vitamins heavily. Cofactors for energy production and neurotransmitter synthesis. Can be stimulating — morning only.
Morning only
Zinc Bisglycinate
15–25mg
Mild deficiency common in active, stressed men. Supports testosterone production, immune function, skin. Bisglycinate form — best absorbed, gentlest on digestion.
With food
Ashwagandha KSM-66
300–600mg
Cortisol reduction, stress resilience, cognitive function under load. KSM-66 is the most clinically studied extract. Cycle 8–12 weeks on, 4 weeks off. Also supports testosterone in stressed men.
Morning
Creatine Monohydrate
5g
Cognitive benefits well-established — improves processing speed and working memory, especially effective when sleep-deprived. Also critical for muscle retention. Any pure monohydrate brand. Anytime.
Lion's Mane
500–1000mg
Stimulates nerve growth factor (NGF) — supports cognitive function, focus, and memory. Emerging evidence for neuroplasticity. Fruiting body extract only (not mycelium on grain). Directly relevant to your brain fog target.
Morning
Ginkgo Biloba
120mg
Improves cerebral blood flow and oxygen delivery to the brain. Good evidence for processing speed, memory, and mental clarity. Mild anticoagulant — avoid if taking blood thinners or aspirin regularly.
